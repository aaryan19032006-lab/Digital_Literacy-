UPI fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes affecting individuals in India. It typically occurs when scammers trick users into sharing sensitive information such as OTPs, UPI PINs, or by sending fraudulent payment requests. In a common scenario, a victim receives a call or message from someone pretending to be a bank official or a customer support agent. The scammer claims that there is an issue with the victim’s account or offers a reward or refund.

The attacker then asks the victim to click on a link or approve a payment request. In some cases, victims are asked to install screen-sharing applications, allowing scammers to gain access to their mobile devices. Once the victim shares their details or approves the request, money is quickly transferred from their account.

Students and elderly individuals are often targeted because they may not be fully aware of such fraud techniques. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and potential misuse of personal information.

This type of fraud highlights the importance of awareness and caution while performing online transactions. Users must understand that legitimate organizations never ask for confidential details like UPI PINs or OTPs.
